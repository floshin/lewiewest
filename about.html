Growing up in Canberra as the youngest of four boys, it’s no wonder Lewis wanted to be a stunt performer. He started his career as a “creative tumbling” online sensation, before “joining the circus”.

After training with Warehouse Circus, Lewis moved to Melbourne to undertake a Bachelor’s Degree from The National Institute of Circus Arts, before joining Circa Contemporary Circus, where he worked for the next seven years.

Lewis has since performed around the world, with highlights including opening the Montreal Circus Festival with Circa, and winning a gold medal in an international circus competition at the Festival Mondial du Cirque de Demain, Paris in 2013.

Lewis joined GOM in 2016, beginning with a regional US tour of A Simple Space, and has been with the company ever since. He's now one of the most senior members of the company, and enjoys his role as a mentor and teacher to new ensemble members.

His most memorable moment on-stage was performing Australia’s first unassisted four-high in GOM’s premiere of Backbone, at the Adelaide Fringe Festival.
